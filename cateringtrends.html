What Are The Most Popular Wedding Catering Trends in India?

Are you planning an extravaganza wedding? Would you prefer sit-down meals or buffets? What about food stations and bars? If you are confused about what to put on your Indian wedding menu then this is the perfect place to start with.

In India, weddings are more than just the union of two people; they are a major celebration of two families coming together, as well as a declaration of position, wealth, and power. It’s no surprise that Indian families spend millions, if not crores, of rupees on elaborate wedding celebrations.

Forget about the various functions, bridal gowns, decor, and bar stock; the food is the most significant component of any great Indian wedding. To make your wedding up with the trends like yourself, we have gathered a selective list of wedding food trends that are going to take hearts and bring water to the mouth of your guests.

Here are top Wedding Reception Catering Trends that you should not miss in 2022:

Old Tastes, New Look:

Dishes that have our hearts since we started walking (some even before). Indians have a separate corner for Pani puri’s, jalebi-rabri whilst having our meals at any wedding. Our inherent desi-ness hasn’t changed, but our methods have. New wedding stalls will see modern presentations for deconstructed chaats, panipuri shooters, keema/kabab burgers, and even dehydrated paan sweets as mouth fresheners. You can consult our wedding planner for more such Wedding Reception Catering Trends that would make your food as extraordinary as it is for you.

Going International with Gourmet:

Long gone are the times when pizza, pasta, or noodles stalls would excite people or even be considered as an “international fusion” on your wedding menu. In order to truly make your wedding unique in terms of catering servings, going gourmet is the way.

Gourmet meals and unique world cuisines such as Mexican, Italian, Japanese, and others are booming in the wedding catering industry. Sushi counters, Korean BBQs, and South American main courses have all made their way onto the Wedding Food Trends in India.

Stay with Roots:

Families are changing their culinary and flavors preferences, but true food enthusiasts are sticking to their traditional roots. People are now displaying numerous regional meals on exhibits, such as live South Indian dosa counters, local Bombay street food like vada pavs, chaats, Gujarati dhoklas, Bihari litti chokhas, and more.

Drinks Vs Fresh Beverages :

While planning a wedding that happens once in a lifetime, why limit the drinks to two sections- hard drinks and soft drinks? You might want to work on the drinks you serve as well! Mocktails such as mojitos, blue lagoons, freshly squeezed juices, and even iced tea give a touch of refinement to your enormous buffet. Moreover, there is a wide variety of beverages to pick from. Each Indian area has its own collection of specialty drinks, and we’ve drawn inspiration from these unique traditions. Narangi Ghonghura, Mogra Sorbet Spritzer, Dhungar Chaas, and Sol Kadhi are some of our clientele’s favorite beverages.
